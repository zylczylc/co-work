<h2>Overview</h2>
<a name="overview"></a>
<div class="overview">
<p><a class="arrow-left-small"></a><a href="https://ucr.fbi.gov/crime-in-the-u.s/2016/crime-in-the-u.s.-2016/tables/table-1/table-1-overview.pdf" target="_self" class="internal-link" title>Download Printable Document</a></p>
<p class=" "><strong>Table 1&#8212;Crime in the United States, by Volume and Rate per 100,000 Inhabitants, 1997&#8211;2016</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>In 2016, the estimated number of violent crime offenses was 1,248,185, an increase of 4.1 percent from the 2015 estimate.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>The violent crime of murder and nonnegligent manslaughter increased 8.6&#160;percent in 2016 when compared with the 2015 estimate. Aggravated assault offenses increased 5.1&#160;percent, and rape offenses (legacy definition) increased 4.9 percent. The violent crime of robbery rose 1.2 percent when compared with the 2015 estimate.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>The 2016 violent crime rate was 386.3 per 100,000 inhabitants, up 3.4 percent when compared with the 2015 violent crime rate.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>The murder rate was 5.3 per 100,000 inhabitants in 2016, a 7.9 percent increase when compared with the estimated rate for the previous year.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>The estimated number of property crimes in 2016 was 7,919,035, a 1.3 percent decrease from the 2015 estimate.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Of the property crimes, the estimated number of burglary offenses decreased 4.6&#160;percent, and larceny-theft offenses declined 1.5 percent. The estimated number of motor vehicle thefts increased 7.4 percent.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>The 2016 property crime rate was 2,450.7 per 100,000, a 2.0 percent decrease when compared with the 2015 rate.</li>
</ul>
